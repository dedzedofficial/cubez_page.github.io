About

You've discovered my sunken treasure.
Dragged up from the ocean floor, this wreck belongs to Our Fish Overlord (aka Ded Zed), a digital trickster and deep-sea meme machine. I float across the net, creating weird, chaotic, and questionably funny content because… why not? If it makes someone laugh, that's gold enough for me.

Welcome aboard.

My time as a metal musician

Ded Zed

Under the alias Ded Zed, I channel distortion and disorder into a sonic punch that leaves a mark. This is for those who crave blast beats, grinding riffs, and vocals torn from the edge. No filters, no frills — just pure catharsis wrapped in metal.

I started as a drummer for my old high school band, Parasitology, where the name Ded Zed originates. From there I evolved into making heavier music, which led me to producing my own material as Ded Zed.

Flowing creativity

Our Fish Overlord

Known as Our Fish Overlord, I make music that's anything but typical for me — yet somehow it resonates. What started as an outlet for game soundtracks quickly evolved into something deeper: a space to explore emotion, identity, and the unexpected paths sound can take.

This journey's just beginning. As I keep pushing boundaries and learning more about myself, the music will grow with me — shifting, expanding, and never standing still.

VR development

From platforms to personal vision

My development journey in VR began in 2022 with Meta Horizons, where I first dipped into world-building — designing social hubs, interactive spaces, and playful micro-experiences. It was my training ground for understanding user presence, avatar expression, and spatial storytelling in three dimensions.

Throughout 2023 and 2024, I was deep in Banter, pushing boundaries with custom scripting and stylized environments. This phase sharpened my technical chops, especially around player interaction, environment logic, and building worlds that felt both expressive and responsive. It was a turning point where I began treating each project as a layered form of creative identity.

By 2025, I expanded into new ecosystems with VRChat and ChilloutVR, embracing the freedom and chaos of community-driven world creation. Alongside these, I began building my own VR game — a fully independent project that fuses immersive gameplay, responsive mechanics, original BGM, and a deeply personal design language. It's not just another game, it's a canvas for everything I've become as a musician, worldbuilder, and digital creator.
